Pitch: Higher and Lower

Pitch is a perceptual property of sound that can be thought of as having a continuous spectrum of values. These values are conventionally taken to be on an up-down axis: one pitch can be higher than, lower than or the same as another pitch.

Organising Pitch

A sequence of pitches forms a melody. Simultaneous pitches give rise to harmony.

Pitches can be grouped into sets (or dictionaries). The choice of which pitch values comprise a set is a matter of individual preference.

Depending on culture, time and place, some pitch sets become more popular than others.

One of the most popular pitch sets consists of 7 pitches: Sa, Re, Ga, Ma, Pa, Dha and Ni.

Pitch sets can be assigned a reference pitch. In the case of the 7-pitch set above, this reference is Sa.

Interval

The distance between two pitches is an interval.

An interval can also be thought of as the relationship that two pitches share.

Octave

An octave is a special interval that symmetrically relates its two constituent pitches.

This symmetry is reflected in the names of pitches that are an octave apart. For example, the pitch situated an octave higher than Sa is named Sa’ (including the apostrophe ‘), and the pitch one octave lower than Ga is named _Ga (including the underscore _).

Scale

Due to octave equivalence, we can think of the entire spectrum of pitch as shifted copies of an octave-limited pitch set. This representative set is called a scale.

Why Dictionaries?

Pitch sets allow us to perceive and remember the pitches that occur in a piece of music, turning the music into a sequence of musical words/sentences.

This is similar to how we use our familiarity with the vowels and consonants of a language to parse what we hear. We’re able to make sense of what is being spoken because of our command of the fundamental sounds.

It’s important to remember that, in order to enjoy music, you don’t really need to have a working knowledge of pitch, or any other element of music. You just need to listen.

At the same time, your enjoyment can be multiplied manifold with a clear sense of pitch. You gain a deep appreciation of the intricacies of sound, and are able to experience a greater depth of emotion while listening to the same piece of music.

The most important reason is to be able to compose music. It’s true that you can enjoy great food without knowing a thing about cooking, but if you want to prepare the dish yourself whenever you like and exactly to your tastes, understanding flavour will take you a long way (not to mention, learning how to cook).
