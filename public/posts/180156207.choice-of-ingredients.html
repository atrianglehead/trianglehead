Renaming Ingredients

Notice that each version of Ma and Dha can be perceived as high or low relative to the other. This allows us to rename the actual pitches based on their relative place in the ordering.

Let’s use the lowercase ma for the lower version of Ma, and the usual uppercase Ma for the higher version of Ma.

By the same logic, we’ll get a lower dha and a higher Dha.

As a result, our ingredients for Melody 1 and Melody 2 will now look like this:

Small Trades

To my ears, depending on which versions of the pitches — ma/Ma or dha/Dha —come together, the flavour of a melody is changing more than what I’d expect from the visual representation alone.

This leads us to another idea: instead of swapping both pairs of pitches, how about swapping out only one?

This leads to 2 new lists of ingredients, and by a complete fluke, they happen to be just the ingredients we need for our two remaining melodies, Melody 3 and Melody 4, from Part 3:
