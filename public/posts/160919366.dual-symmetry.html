In Part 6, we saw how octaves generate symmetry in the pitch domain. This gave us a good reason to use Sa-octaves as pitch references.

Tempo-beats play a similar role in the time domain. While we’ve already seen them in action, we haven’t yet seen how they’re different compared to the other beats that we have available.

By breaking down time into multiple “views”, let’s take a look at the symmetries that tempo-beats generate.

Duration-Interval Parallels

In Part 6, we explored Sa-octave symmetries by looking at the pitch contour of a sequence of pitches.

We saw that octave-shifted versions, when overlaid, retained the pitch contour in both views: Front View and Top View. Shifting by any other interval left the Front View contour unchanged, but changed the Top View contour.

To see how tempo-beats create natural symmetries, we looked at the rhythmic contour of a sequence of beats.

We saw how tempo-beat shifted versions, when overlaid, retained the rhythm contour in both views: Front View and Side View. Shifting by any other duration left the Front View contour unchanged, but changed the Side View contour.
