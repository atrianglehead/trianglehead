Order Matters

If you want to have a glass of lemonade, it can make quite a difference when you squeeze the lemon into the glass. Here’s one option:

Get an empty glass → Add water → Squeeze in the lemon → Add sugar → Drink → Discard glass

Here’s another:

Get an empty glass → Add water → Add sugar → Squeeze in the lemon → Drink → Discard glass

But if you do this…

Get an empty glass → Add water → Drink → Squeeze in the lemon → Add sugar → Discard glass

…you would just end up drinking a glass of water.

Order matters.

But in what order? Is it Sa-Ga or Ga-Sa?1

This might seem really obvious, but any time you have two non-overlapping sounds in succession, then one of them is heard before the other. Or you could say that one of them is heard after the other.

In the pitch domain, we have lower and higher. In the domain of time, we have before and after (or earlier and later).

The order of pitches changes a melody, sometimes quite drastically.

Ranking Durations

V and W are different because of their durations. Is it possible to rank them?

Certainly. And the good news is that most people find ranking durations to be much easier than ranking pitches.

The sound that lasts for a longer time is by definition the one with the longer duration. In our example, W has a longer duration than V, so we can call it the longer sound.

Put another way, V is the shorter sound.
