How well can you comprehend the essence of the melody below?

In the Decoding Melody series, we worked our way towards a framework for decoding melodies using values for 3 melodic elements: pitch, time and loudness.

You can find a quick overview of the Decoding Melody series through the link below:

New Frontiers

Our framework was robust enough to help us decode some existing melodies. And yet, it was limited to using a narrow range of values for each of the 3 elements:

  • Pitch: 7 values (Sa, Re, Ga, Ma, Pa, Dha and Ni)

  • Time: 4 values (Ta, Ka, Di and Mi)

  • Loudness: 3 values (Ve, Ya and Ha)

As it stands now, this model doesn’t possess the breadth to handle a melody like the one you heard above, or even the countless melodies you might come across in existing songs.

We’ll change that, one step at a time, by enriching our framework with more nuance.

This will require us to engage in a deeper study of each element and the possibilities contained within.

The first element we’ll be exploring is pitch. Specifically, we’ll be listening to (and looking at) collections of pitches, called scales.

Onwards

Developing an intuition for scales can be a game-changer in the process of decoding melodies.

Beginning with the next part, we’ll see how the melody you heard earlier (along with others) can be understood if we expand our model of pitch.

The goal is to decipher a wider range of melodies. Evolving our conception of pitch is simply a means to that end.
