Extension to Longer Durations

With our current naming scheme, both of these would be decoded as TaKaDiMi.

Let’s change that. Whenever we pass by a tempo beat without specifically saying Ta, we’ll extend the vowel of the previous syllable. With this small change, the two rhythms above would get separate identities:

The first remains TaKaDiMi.

The second becomes Ta aKa aDi iMi. Each vowel that we’ve added is just an extension of the immediately preceding syllable.

For simplicity, we’ll use the hyphen (-) to represent any vowel extension. This will turn the first two rhythms in this section into:

  • TaKaDiMi → TaKaDiMi (unchanged)

  • Ta aKa aDi iMi → Ta -Ka -Di -Mi

Measured Waiting

Now we get to the actual challenges! Each challenge will only contain some combination of 3 sub-rhythms or phrases. These 3 rhythms (in increasing order of length) are:

  • TaDiMi

  • Ta TaKa

  • Ta -DiMi

The main difference in the rhythmic contour of these 3 phrases is the increasing gap between the first and second beat. By the third phrase, we’re waiting for quite a long time before we hear the two beats at the end.
