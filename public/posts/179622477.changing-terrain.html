Ok, Now What?

Now that we have the visual representations of each of these melodies, I need to tell you something (if you haven’t already noticed).

Within the context of any one of the above melodies, the names and representations of pitches work perfectly.

But looking at all 4 melodies together, the very same pitch names and representations are misleading. Put another way, the representations of pitch in the first 3 pictures above are not entirely correct.

Now, don’t be mad. Let me explain.

This wasn’t something I did to mislead you. On the contrary, I’ve used the same names and representations found in the Decoding Melody series:

What went wrong then?

The problem is that when we try to use 7 pitch names (Sa, Re, Ga, Ma, Pa, Dha and Ni) for all 4 melodies, we’re forced to use the same name for multiple pitches.

For example, the Ma of Melody 1 and the Ma of Melody 4 are not the same pitch.

You’d be right to think that this creates a confusing situation. So, what can we do about it?

While you ponder that, I have another question for you:

In the context of our 4 melodies, can you identify all pitch names that refer to multiple pitches?

You already know that Ma refers to one pitch in Melody 1 and an entirely different one in Melody 4. How many other pitch names are doing this kind of multi-speak?
