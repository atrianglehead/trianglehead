How can we tell one word from another in Morse code? Morse code has just two elements: a short sound and a long sound.

Here’s the short sound:

[audio]

And here’s the long one:

[audio]

Using just these two sounds, we can get all 26 letters of the English alphabet. In writing, the short sound is represented by a dot (.) and the long sound by a dash (-).

Here’s a table of Morse code:

[photo]

The difference between the two elements of Morse code, the dot and the dash, is their duration. The dash is longer than the dot.

What does Morse code have to do with rhythm? In a sense, everything.

If we forget about the correspondence of Morse code with English letters, and instead just listen to them as meaningless sounds, we can easily transition to the world of rhythm.
