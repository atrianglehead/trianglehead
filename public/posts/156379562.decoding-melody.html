Melodic Phrases

If you’re having a conversation with someone, it’s important that the two of you take turns speaking. That way you can comprehend and respond to what the other person is saying.

[audio]

If you’re both talking simultaneously the entire time, it’s safe to say that it’s no longer a conversation. It’s not all bad though. This is a highly prized skill in news debate panels.

[audio]

A more democratic conversation, where only one of you is speaking at any given time, is somewhat like a musical melody, where one voice or instrument communicates with sequential, non-overlapping sounds.

[audio]

Each sentence in your ideal conversation would be the equivalent of a melodic phrase. So the phrase you heard at the start of this post could be something that you have “said” in your musical language.

Elements of Melody

The two elements of melody that are usually highlighted are:

  1. pitch

  2. rhythm

There are others, but these two tend to be the most noticeable aspects of melody. In my experience, paying attention to the elements of melody helps with musical fluency. Understanding and remembering musical phrases becomes much easier.

We’ll first explore pitch in this series of posts.

I should mention though, that the great thing about music, unlike spoken language, is that you can actually have great conversations even when many of you communicate simultaneously. Together, multiple voices can create what is known as harmony. A news panellist’s nightmare!
