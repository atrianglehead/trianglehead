Intentional Viewing

To make the most of the process, I’d recommend following these steps for each composition:

  1. Allow your eyes to simply scan it the first time.

  2. Pause. Deepen your gaze, slow down, and observe what you feel and think.

  3. Take a breath. If there was something that stood out in the composition, think about why it felt that way.

Ask yourself these questions for each composition:

  1. What is the most memorable aspect of the composition and why?

  2. What is the feeling you associate with the entire composition?

  3. How did the overall context of the composition change how you perceived the common hook-on-a-square visual element?

Scaling New Heights

Now that we’ve explored one basic idea (one word/phrase/element in different contexts) in 3 different contexts (poetry, music and visuals), I’d like to ask you a question:

Given a few emotionally expressive artworks (in any form: music, poetry, painting, etc.), how would you identify and organise its elements in such a way that the relationships between elements and emotional expression became clear to you?

In other words, from a set of artworks, how would you extract a framework linking artistic elements to emotional expression?

Scales are a partial answer to this question, when the element is pitch and the art form is music.

We’ll soon see how.
