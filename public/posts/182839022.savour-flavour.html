Ingredient → Flavour, Pitch → Mood

Ingredients contribute to the flavour profile of a dish; colours to the mood of a painting; and pitches to the mood of a song.

In preceding parts of the Scale series, we related scales to our experience of mood. In this part, we’ll further explore this connection by listening to and looking at a few scales.

You can deepen your experience of listening to the examples that follow, by being aware of how various pitch combinations feel to you.

After all, our primary goal is not to have a purely theoretical or visual framework, but to actually experience embodied music.

Laser Focus

In Part 11, we constrained our idea of a scale in multiple ways, and also narrowed our focus to scales that contain only 5 or 7 pitches (excluding the higher Sa' of course).

5-pitch scales are generally called pentatonic or audav, while 7-pitch scales are called heptatonic or sampūrna.

In order to more easily perceive the mood of each scale example, I’ve paired every scale with a short, representative melody. In each audio, you’ll first hear a scale, then a short pause, followed by a melody based on the scale.

Only for music nerds: In a scale’s circular representation, notice the various intervals (represented by different combinations of black and white lines) that connect adjacent pitches. This interval pattern is an important way to fingerprint scales. The non-nerds will find out about this later on, so don’t tell them just yet. Shhh!
